# About Stephan Bökelmann

Stephan Bökelmann is an engineer, physicist, and consultant based in Bochum, Germany. His work spans the full vertical stack of engineering — from silicon-level measurement systems and embedded firmware up through monitoring infrastructure, data platforms, and application-layer software.

## Background

Stephan holds a Master of Engineering and conducts research at the **Institut für Experimentalphysik I** at Ruhr-Universität Bochum. There he contributes to the **PANDA luminosity detector** experiment at [FAIR](https://fair-center.eu) (Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research) in Darmstadt — one of the largest particle accelerator projects currently under construction.

His engineering career began in 2007 in physical measurement technology in the chemical industry, followed by work at **CERN** and at **DB Netz AG**, where he developed microcontroller- and ASIC-based measurement systems for critical rail infrastructure. In 2014 he joined **AUTO INTERN**, and together with Odin Holmes transformed the company into a sought-after contract developer for process diagnostics in industry and science.

He is a scientific staff member at **THGA Bochum** (Technische Hochschule Georg Agricola), where he lectures, mentors students, and builds supplementary teaching material for courses covering programming, object-oriented design, and databases.

## Community

**emBO++** — Stephan is the principal organizer of the International Technical Symposium for Embedded Systems Development, held annually in Bochum. The conference brings together engineers from across Europe for deep-technical talks on modern C++ patterns for embedded and real-time systems. Notable past presentations include sessions at Shenzhen (2025) and a KiCon keynote filmed in Bochum (2025).

**Bochum AI-Gruppe** — he co-leads this practitioner group together with Odin Holmes, running meetups and workshops at the intersection of machine learning, measurement systems, and industry applications.

He is also a member of the **examination board of the Chamber of Commerce** for IT specialists.
